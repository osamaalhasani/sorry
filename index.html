I'm Sorry, My Love

My dearest,

As I sit here trying to find the words to express how truly sorry I am, I realize that no combination of letters could ever fully capture the weight of regret I carry in my heart. But I owe you this attempt—this honest reflection of my shortcomings and the pain I've caused you.

I've been blind to the ways my actions have hurt you. In my own selfish moments, I failed to see how my words cut deep, how my inattention made you feel invisible, and how my stubbornness built walls between us instead of bridges. For all of this, I am profoundly sorry.

You have always given me your whole heart—honest, vulnerable, and beautiful. While I held parts of myself back, afraid of my own vulnerabilities. You deserved better than the half-version of love I offered when I was too caught in my own struggles to be fully present with you.

What hurts most is knowing I dimmed the light in your eyes—that special spark that first drew me to you. The one I promised myself I would always protect. Instead, I watched it flicker under the shadow of my mistakes, and for that, my heart breaks a thousand times over.

I want you to know that I see you now—truly see you. I see your strength in setting boundaries, your courage in speaking your truth, and your grace in giving me space to reflect. You have always been the stronger one between us.

I'm not asking for immediate forgiveness. I know trust rebuilds slowly, like the first tender shoots after a long winter. What I am asking for is a chance to show you, through consistent actions rather than just these words, that I can grow, that I can be better, that I can love you the way you've always deserved to be loved.

You are the poetry in my ordinary life, the color in my grayscale world. Meeting you wasn't just luck—it was the universe giving me a gift I didn't know how to properly treasure. But I want to learn, if you'll let me.

Whether we find our way back to each other or not, please know that you have changed me in ways that will remain. You've shown me what it means to love deeply and what it feels like to be truly seen. These are lessons I will carry with me always.

With all my heart,
Osama
